## [Unreleased]

### Changed
- **Per-sale ennakkovero calculator is now MSFT-only in the UI** ‚Äî the tax-calculation button on the Transaction History page now appears only for `MSFT` sell rows (a `TAX_FILING_SYMBOLS` frontend constant mirroring the backend `tax_filing_required` gate). MSFT is the only holding that requires a manual Finnish ennakkovero filing (it is a Fidelity ESPP position reported in the US); every other holding sits at Nordnet, which calculates and withholds Finnish capital-gains tax automatically, so showing a manual calculator for them was misleading.
- **Redesigned the bracket section of the tax dialog** ‚Äî replaced the inline 30 %/34 % note with a dedicated `BracketCard`: a stacked bracket bar (prior YTD income ¬∑ this sale's 30 % part ¬∑ this sale's 34 % part) with a dashed ‚Ç¨30,000 threshold marker, a color-coded breakdown listing each part with its own tax amount, the total ennakkovero for the sale, and a green headroom line when the sale stays inside the 30 % band. The header text states plainly whether the whole sale, part of it, or none of it falls into the 34 % bracket.
- **Frontend hosting** ‚Äî migrated React frontend from Azure Container Apps (nginx) to Azure Static Web Apps (Free tier). The frontend now calls the backend cross-origin via the `VITE_API_BASE_URL` build-time variable; the auth cookie uses `SameSite=None; Secure`. Cuts hosting cost by ~‚Ç¨19/month and adds a global CDN. CI builds with `VITE_API_BASE_URL` from a GitHub repo Variable and deploys via `azure/static-web-apps-deploy@v1`. The old `portfolio-frontend` Container App, its nginx proxy, and the frontend Dockerfile have been removed.

### Added
- **Ennakkovero declaration & payment tracking (MSFT)** ‚Äî saved per-sale tax calculations can now be marked **declared & paid** in OmaVero, with the **actual amount and date** the user paid recorded (`declared_at`, `paid_amount_eur`, `paid_date` on `tax_calculations`). The per-row tax-statement icon is now a traffic light: **muted** (no calculation), **amber** (saved but not yet declared/paid), **green** (declared & paid). A new **"Ennakkovero ‚Äî declared vs remaining (MSFT)"** card on the Transaction History page shows, per year, the **total** advance tax across the year's MSFT sales split into **declared** vs **still-to-declare**, a progress bar, an **OmaVero annual summary** (total luovutushinnat, hankintamenot, luovutusvoitot/-tappiot), a per-sale list with a one-click declared toggle and an **expandable per-sale OmaVero panel** (luovutushinta, hankintameno, luovutusvoitto/-tappio, ennakkovero) so the return can be filled without opening each calculation, and a **paid-vs-computed reconciliation** that flags any over/under-payment versus the (possibly corrected) computed figure. The **"still to pay"** headline is **payment-aware**: it nets what you've **actually paid** against the year's total liability (`remaining_to_pay = total_tax ‚àí total_paid`), so an overpayment on an earlier sale reduces what's left to pay on later sales rather than charging the raw computed tax on top ‚Äî you pay exactly enough to even out the year, no excess. If you've paid more than the whole year's liability the card shows the over-coverage (refunded at the year-end assessment). Because the per-sale figures are marginal and stack chronologically, they sum exactly to the year's total prepayment, so "remaining" is mathematically correct. The sale's tax dialog gains a declaration panel for entering the exact paid amount/date. New `PATCH /transactions/tax-calculations/{id}/declaration` and `GET /transactions/tax-calculations/declaration-summary?year=&symbol=` endpoints; summary aggregation is a pure, unit-tested service (`app/services/declarations.py`). New nullable columns are applied automatically on startup (Postgres `ADD COLUMN IF NOT EXISTS`) and via Alembic migration `e5c1a7f3b2d8`.
- **Automatic 30 %/34 % bracket in the per-sale ennakkovero calculation**‚Äî the tax calculationfor a sold stock (`GET /transactions/tax-calculation`) now automatically positions the sale's gain on the per-year ‚Ç¨30,000 capital-income bracket using the user's capital income realised **earlier in the same calendar year** ‚Äî i.e. gains and 85 %-taxable dividends dated **strictly before this sale** (OST excluded), computed directly from stored transactions, no manual re-entry. Chronological stacking means the first sales of the year fill the 30 % band first and later sales cross into 34 % once the year-to-date total passes ‚Ç¨30,000. The tax math is marginal: `bracket_total_tax(prior + gain) ‚àí bracket_total_tax(prior)`, so a net year-to-date capital **loss** correctly shields the gain and an already-over-threshold year taxes the whole sale at 34 %. A hypothetical (unsaved) QuickTrade sale naturally stacks on top of all income before its date. The response gains a `bracket` object (prior YTD income, threshold, headroom, and the 30 %/34 % split of this sale), and the dialog shows a prominent amber warning when part or all of the sale is taxed at 34 % (or a green "stays in the 30 % band, ‚Ç¨X headroom left" note otherwise). Untracked capital income (rental, interest, non-listed dividends) still counts toward the same ‚Ç¨30k and is caveated in the notes.
- **Year-to-date capital-income tracker (‚Ç¨30k bracket)**‚Äî a new card on the Transaction History page shows how much **taxable capital income** has accumulated in the year versus the ‚Ç¨30,000 Finnish capital-income bracket threshold, so it's easy to see how close the year is to the higher 34 % rate. It is computed **directly from stored transactions** (no saved tax calculations required) by a new pure, unit-tested service (`app/services/capital_income.py`) behind `GET /transactions/capital-income-summary?year=`. Realized capital gains use the same per-lot best-of hankintameno-olettama engine as the ennakkovero calculation, with **per-account FIFO** cost basis. **Dividends** are now included at **85 % taxable** (TVL 33a ¬ß, 15 % tax-free); accumulating ETFs (e.g. SXR8) have no dividend rows so they're naturally excluded. **OST accounts (`tax_treatment = deferred`)** are excluded entirely ‚Äî both gains and dividends are taxed only on withdrawal ‚Äî and the card surfaces the excluded OST amounts. Capital losses net against the combined income before the 30 %/34 % bracket is applied. The card shows a progress bar, remaining 30 %-band headroom (or amount over the threshold), a gains / taxable-dividends / estimated-tax breakdown, and a year selector. (Replaces the earlier saved-calculation-only `GET /transactions/tax-calculations/summary` endpoint.) Note: rental, interest, non-listed dividends and other untracked capital income also count toward the same ‚Ç¨30k limit but are not included.
- **Delete / re-run saved tax calculations** ‚Äî added the ability to remove saved ennakkovero calculations so they can be re-run after a logic correction. New `DELETE /transactions/tax-calculations/{id}` (single) and `DELETE /transactions/tax-calculations/` (bulk, optional `symbol`/`year` filters) endpoints. The Transaction History page now shows a per-row trash icon next to each saved sell calculation and a "Delete tax calcs (N)" button in the header (both confirm first). Saving is now **idempotent**: re-running the same sale replaces its previous calculation (matched by linked transaction, else by symbol + sell date + quantity) instead of creating a duplicate row.
- **Automatic USD‚ÜíEUR FX conversion on import** ‚Äî Fidelity ESPP statements are USD-native, so imported transactions previously kept raw USD figures in their EUR fields until the user manually ran `POST /transactions/fix-fx-rates/{symbol}`. The Fidelity upload now converts every transaction to EUR automatically using the historical ECB reference rate for each transaction's own date (frankfurter.app), so cost basis and the ennakkovero calculation are tax-ready immediately. The conversion is extracted into a reusable, unit-tested service (`app/services/fx.py`); the manual endpoint now reuses it as a re-run/fallback. The conversion is best-effort ‚Äî an FX-API outage logs a warning and leaves the import intact (re-run the endpoint later). The upload response includes an `fx_conversion` summary.
- **Cookie-based password gate** ‚Äî simple shared-secret auth replacing MSAL for personal use. No more Azure AD token acquisition on page load; login is a single password prompt stored as an HTTP-only cookie.
- **Keep-alive cron** ‚Äî GitHub Actions workflow pings the backend every 5 minutes (the shortest reliable GH Actions interval) to prevent Container Apps cold starts.

### Fixed
- **Dividend auto-detection no longer books unpaid dividends early** ‚Äî the yfinance dividend detector (`app/services/dividend_detector.py`) keyed off the **ex-dividend date** and recorded a dividend as income as soon as that date passed, even though the cash is not paid until weeks later (yfinance exposes only the ex-date, never the pay date). This booked declared-but-unpaid dividends prematurely ‚Äî e.g. MSFT's 2026 Q2 dividend ($0.91/share, ex-date 2026-05-21, **payable 2026-06-11**) was recorded on 2026-05-21. The detector now estimates the pay date as `ex-date + PAYMENT_DELAY_DAYS` (21 days, ‚âà the US large-cap ex‚Üípay gap) and only records a dividend once that estimated pay date is on or before today, storing the transaction at the estimated pay date so the income falls in the correct tax year. The recognition window (`LOOKBACK_DAYS`) was widened from 30 to 60 days so the daily scheduler reliably catches each dividend once its pay date arrives, and deduplication now matches within a ¬±7-day window (absorbing the pay-date approximation and small date differences against manually imported rows). Auto-detected dividend rows now store `quantity = 0` (a dividend is income, not a share movement) with the per-share figure and share count preserved in the notes. The decision logic is extracted into a pure, unit-tested module (`app/services/dividend_logic.py`).
- **Capital-income tracker now ignores dividend-withholding rows** ‚Äî the Nordnet importer historically mapped both `OSINKO` (gross dividend, +) and `ENNAKKOPID√ÑTYS` (advance withholding, ‚àí) to the `dividend` transaction type, so naively summing dividend rows yielded a **net** figure. The ‚Ç¨30k tracker (`app/services/capital_income.py`) now skips non-positive dividend rows (withholding is prepaid tax, a foreign-tax credit, not income), so it counts the **gross** listed-share dividend at 85 %. The importer (`import_nordnet_history.py`) no longer imports `ENNAKKOPID√ÑTYS` as a dividend; the Fidelity importer (`import_fidelity_history.py`) now also emits a gross `dividend` income row alongside each DRIP reinvestment `buy` (MSFT dividends are auto-reinvested, so the taxable income was previously invisible ‚Äî recorded as 0-share rows that don't affect cost basis). Verified against Fidelity statements (e.g. 2026 Q1 MSFT $541.31 gross, $81.20 US withholding) and Nordnet CSVs (`OSINKO` Summa = qty √ó per-share = gross).
- **Restored the `GET /by-transaction/{id}` tax-calculation route** ‚Äî the decorator on `get_tax_calculation_by_transaction` was accidentally dropped when the DELETE endpoints were added, leaving the function unregistered (the endpoint would 404). The decorator is restored. (No live UI used it, so there was no user-visible breakage.)
- **"Failed to delete" error on a successful delete** ‚Äî the frontend API helper unconditionally parsed the response body as JSON, but `DELETE /transactions/tax-calculations/{id}` returns `204 No Content` (empty body). Deleting a saved tax calculation succeeded on the backend but threw a JSON-parse error in the UI, showing a misleading "Failed to delete" toast. `request()` now returns early for `204`/empty bodies.
- **QuickTrade USD-before-rate guard** ‚Äî submitting a USD trade via the Enter key could bypass the disabled submit button and pass the raw USD price through as EUR (the `priceEur` fallback) when the sale-day EUR/USD rate hadn't loaded yet, inflating the cost basis / ennakkovero. `handleSubmit` now blocks USD trades (and USD fees) until the rate is available and shows a "waiting for exchange rate" toast.
- **Ennakkovero 10-year boundary now calendar-based**‚Äî the hankintameno-olettama rate (40 % for lots held ‚â•10 years, 20 % otherwise) is now determined by calendar arithmetic (the lot's exact 10-year anniversary date) instead of a `days / 365.25` approximation, which could misclassify a lot by ~1 day right at the boundary and flip the deemed-cost rate. Leap-day lots (Feb 29) fall back to Feb 28 in a non-leap target year. Extracted into pure helpers `ten_year_anniversary()` / `held_at_least_10_years()` in `app/services/tax.py` with regression tests (`tests/test_holding_period.py`).
- **Ennakkovero guidance notes corrected** ‚Äî replaced the incorrect "file an ennakkoveroilmoitus within 2 months of the sale" note with the correct lis√§ennakko (additional prepayment) guidance: pay in MyTax without interest by the end of January following the tax year (e.g. a 2026 sale ‚Üí by 31 Jan 2027), minimum ‚Ç¨170, with relief interest accruing thereafter. Added notes surfacing the ‚Ç¨1,000 small-disposals exemption (TVL 48.6 ¬ß, a per-year aggregate the calculator cannot see) and clarifying that the 30 %/34 % bracket depends on the taxpayer's whole-year capital income (dividends, rental, other gains) and deductible losses ‚Äî verified against the Finnish Tax Administration's guidance and the KPMG "Taxation of the Stock Awards in FI" memo.
- **Ennakkovero per-lot hankintameno-olettama** ‚Äî the capital-gains tax calculation(`/transactions/tax-calculation`) now applies the deemed acquisition cost (hankintameno-olettama) **per lot** by each lot's own holding period (40 % for lots held ‚â•10 years, 20 % otherwise) and lets each lot independently choose the cheaper of actual vs. deemed cost. Previously a single recent lot forced the whole sale to the 20 % rate (`all_over_10` bug), overstating the taxable gain and overpaying tax (~‚Ç¨2,400 on a representative mixed-lot MSFT sale). Added a lot-coverage guard: if recorded buy lots cover fewer shares than were sold, the shortfall shares now get the 20 % olettama and a loud warning instead of a silently understated cost basis. The math is extracted into a pure, unit-tested module (`app/services/tax.py`) with regression tests (`tests/test_tax.py`). Output adds `hankintameno_kaytetty` (effective deduction), a `coverage` block, and per-lot `applied_deemed_rate`/`method`; `recommended_method` may now be `yhdistelma` (mixed).
- **FIFO cost basis** ‚Äî switched P/L calculation from average cost to FIFO (First In, First Out), matching Fidelity and Finnish tax rules. Previously, selling old cheap ESPP lots didn't properly remove their low cost basis, overstating unrealized P/L by ~‚Ç¨21k.
- **Dashboard 500 error** ‚Äî removed shadowed `JSONResponse` import that caused the combined dashboard endpoint to crash.
- **Mobile card truncation** ‚Äî summary cards (PortfolioSummary, DailySummary, CashAvailable) no longer truncate values on narrow viewports.
- **Stale earnings calendar** ‚Äî cached earnings data now filters out past dates at response time, and frontend guards against negative `daysUntil` values. Previously, 24h-cached data could show yesterday's earnings after midnight.

### Changed
- **Auth simplification** ‚Äî removed MSAL authentication entirely; the app now uses a lightweight cookie-based password gate. This eliminates the Azure AD app registration dependency and speeds up page load significantly.
- **Container Apps scaling** ‚Äî reverted to `min_replicas: 0` (scale-to-zero) with a GitHub Actions keep-alive cron instead of always-on instances, saving ~‚Ç¨17/month.

### Changed
